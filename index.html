Hello Everyone!

I am Yanqiao Wang

Wang Yanqiao graduated at the Central Conservatory of Music (CCOM) in Beijing, under the tutelage of renowned Professors Wu Zuqiang and Jiang Dingxian. A member of the Chinese Association of Musicians, and the North American Association of Composers, he is a visiting professor of CCOM, and emeritus professor of Xinjiang Arts University. Before going abroad, he was a composer in residence for the China National Symphony Orchestra, where he, Wu Zuqiang, Du Mingxin and others together composed the famous Chinese ballet "The Red Detachment of Women"; and he, Wu Zuqiang and Liu Dehai composed a pipa concerto "Prairie Little Sisters". He also composed soundtrack for the movies “Red Pannier”, "Large Running River ", "Drizzly Spring Rain", "Wedding" etc. In 1980 Mr. Wang went to Japan to become a fellow of the Japan International Cultural Exchange Foundation where he delivered lectures on "Comparison of the Chinese and the Okinawan folk tune styles"; while at Keio University he worked and presented a research study report on the Chinese minority folk tune style. Mr. Wang became professor and deputy dean of the Japan International Academy of Music, and he was also the music director of the Japanese "Rope Seat" and "Ghost Dance" theatre companies. His works include: "Shima" "Journey to the West", "Son of the Sun", "Sohrab White Horse", etc. He also produced the Japanese shakuhachi and koto duo (premiered by a famous Japanese shakuhachi player Kifu Mitsuhashi), "Morning in Dongzhai," "Ambush on All Sides" (played by Japanese Music Group) and other works. The Tokyo Casals Hall had held the" Wang YanQiao music concerts. " Immigrated to Canada in 1991, Mr. Wang continued in music composing, teaching and conducting. The Toronto and Ottawa Symphony Orchestras had played his New Year overture. During the last century, Mr. Wang’s works include the ballet "Ancient Fantasy", erhu concerto "Melody of a Hundred Birds", chamber music "Artux Folksong", and choral works "Three Character Classic", " Nostalgia", "Ah! China" and so on. In recent years, apart from giving lectures in Beijing, Xinjiang, Wuhan and other places, Mr. Wang composed and conducted national choral concerts in Xinjiang; composed and directed large-scale symphonic visual concerts "Beautiful Country" in Hebei. For the CCOM Anniversary celebration concert, he composed six piano pieces and directed the performance by 12 famous piano professors, including Zhou Guangren, Guo Zhihong, Li Qifang, Chen Bigang, Wu Ying and others. In March 2011, Mr. Wang’s large-scale symphonic visual concert “Sound of Taihe” was performed by the National Symphony Orchestra, conducted by Li Xincao in a CCTV televised concert at the China National Grand Theatre. In late 2014, Mr. Wang was commissioned by Toronto’s New Music Concerts to compose a chamber music piece – Guizhou Qing Shui Jiang at Night – for their concert by Chinese and Canadian composers held in February 2015. Recently, a Toronto concert has just premiered his new work on a viola concerto in October this year. In 2013 and early 2014, he was commissioned by the Cultural Federation of Hainan Province to compose the "Coconut Island Charm - Hainan Folk Choral Suite" which will be premiered in Hainan at the end of 2015. In addition to composing and teaching, Mr. Wang, with a strong interest in music theory, has spent lots of efforts on studying folk and primitive music and of their relationship to the peoples and cultures around several regions in China. Since 1960, he has travelled deep into the country sites for special investigative study and research, covering places like Xinjiang, Guizhou, Guangdong, Hainan, Shaanxi, Shanxi, Henan, Hebei, Hubei, Hunan, etc. Through his collection and analytical study of folk music, he is able to compose his Chinese music in folk style, rhythm, structure and timbre with unique ethnomusicology.
